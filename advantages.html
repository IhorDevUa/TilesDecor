Hardness +

Keramic tiles are known for their durability and wear resistance. It can withstand heavy loads and is not subject to deformation from pressure or wear. The hardness of ceramic tiles is usually evaluated using different scales, such as the size and density of the crystals, the porosity of the material and its structure. It is determined mainly by the material from which it is made and the technology of its production. The main characteristics of the hardness of ceramic tiles include:

  • Material: Ceramic tiles can be made from different materials such as clay, porcelain, or ceramic. Each of these materials has its hardness properties.
  • Structure: If a ceramic tile has thicker crystal layers or a higher density, it will likely have a higher level of hardness.
  • Porosity: A porous structure can reduce the hardness of the tile, as the pores can weaken its structure.
  • Glazing: Glazed tiles often have a higher hardness due to the finishing process.
  • Classification of hardness. There are various methods of measuring hardness, such as the Mohs scale, which uses the hardness of minerals to classify materials by hardness.
